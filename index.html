About me

I'm a Ph.D. Candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the West Virginia University. I want to play a significant role in the process of creating and transferring knowledge through research and teaching. My professional objective is to pursue a research career at a university or in a research organization.

My research interests lie in the area of Health Informatics (such as Mortality, Obesity, Biological Age Prediction), machine learning, Deep learning, Survival Models (e.g. CoxPH, KM plots) and Biological Vs. Chronological Age.

My work experience includes integrating National Health (CDC data) with survival analysis and also study the chronological and biological age of a person.

I work voluntarily at a prestigious conference SBP (International Conference on Social Computing, Behavioral-Cultural Modeling, & Prediction and Behavior Representation in Modeling and Simulation). I have worked as the student chair in 2015.
